## Literacy Tutoring and Workforce Model

### Summary

The READ USA Literacy Tutoring and Workforce Development model pairs trained teenage (and early college) tutors with elementary students reading below grade level. Tutors work as paid staff, delivering one-on-one literacy support supervised by certified reading teachers. For more information, visit [readusainc.com/literacy-tutoring-program](https://www.readusainc.com/literacy-tutoring-program).

### Core Approach

READ USA trains and employs teenagers — primarily from under-resourced communities — to provide daily, intensive, one-on-one literacy tutoring to elementary students from those same communities. Teen tutors receive an intensive two-day training in literacy skills at the start of their employment, earn $15/hour, and receive ongoing training sessions and real-time coaching from certified reading teachers throughout the program. The model is intentionally bidirectional: it builds literacy skills in elementary-aged students while simultaneously developing workforce skills and professional experience in teen tutors. The program typically operates as an after-school model. *(www.readusainc.com/literacy-tutoring-program)*

### Resources Provided

READ USA provides curriculum and instructional materials, assessment and feedback tools, professional learning and coaching support, staffing support, family engagement and collaboration resources, and transportation and nutrition support (B&O). *(www.readusainc.com/)*

### Impact

READ USA reports that students who participated in the Literacy Tutoring program were 68% more likely to increase a performance level in reading skills compared to students in the control group. For more details, visit [readusainc.com](https://www.readusainc.com/).

### Cost & Access

Cost is associated with this program. Specific pricing is not publicly listed. For details, visit [readusainc.com](https://www.readusainc.com/).

### PD Requirements

Provider professional development is required. PD and coaching support are central to implementing the model — teen tutors receive an intensive initial training plus ongoing coaching, and certified reading teachers supervise implementation. For details, visit [readusainc.com](https://www.readusainc.com/).

### Technology Needs

1:1 devices are required. For specific technical requirements, visit [readusainc.com](https://www.readusainc.com/).

### Scheduling Impact

Schedule flexibility is required. The program is designed as an after-school model, requiring dedicated after-school scheduling structures to be set up for both elementary tutees and teen tutors.

### Off-Site Learning

Off-site learning is required. Teen tutors work at elementary school sites to deliver tutoring; depending on implementation, this may require travel from a different school or location. *(www.readusainc.com/literacy-tutoring-program)*

### Partnerships

Partnerships are required. The model requires coordination between the high school or program serving teen tutors and the elementary schools where tutoring is delivered. *(www.readusainc.com/)*

### Family Involvement

Family involvement depends on implementation. Transportation for teen tutors to the tutoring site may need to be arranged by families depending on proximity and local transportation support available.

### Data Sharing

Data sharing requirements are not confirmed. For details, visit [readusainc.com](https://www.readusainc.com/).
